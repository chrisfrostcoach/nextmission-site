ABT-02 / ONE NAME ON THE ROLL

One name is already on this roll. Mine. I'm not asking you to do anything I didn't do first.

I did my time in uniform, handed my kit back, and walked into the same quiet most of us walk into. The paperwork calls it resettlement. What it actually was: the mission gone, the section scattered, and a standard I'd carried for years with nowhere left to point it. Nobody re-issued any of it. I drifted — not off a cliff, by degrees. The capability was all still there. It had just lost its orders.

So I went and got the orders back. Twenty-odd years building businesses since, teams led under real pressure, and a six-figure sum of my own into the best coaching and performance training I could find — the figure's on the record above, and it went there because I refuse to guess with other men's lives. What changed wasn't a transformation. It was a change of state: a mission again, a standard again, and men around me who'd hold the line. The Next Mission is that, written down as plain orders — the manual I wish someone had handed me with my discharge papers.

ABT-03 / WHY I BUILT THIS

In his own words

I served, and then I left, and the thing nobody puts in the resettlement pack is that the hardest fight starts after the handshake. I've watched the drift take men I served with — capable men — not in one dramatic collapse but by degrees: the standard slipping, the phone going quiet, the mission replaced by a job, and the job by nothing much. I've built businesses for more than twenty years since, led teams under pressure, and faced my own version of that drift. I coach from lived experience — not from a textbook.

I've put over £200,000 of my own money into training with the world's leading coaches and performance psychologists. Not to collect certificates — because I refuse to guess with other men's lives. That investment exists so you don't have to make it: everything in the programme was tested on me first, stripped of the theatre, and written down in plain orders.

The Next Mission is the manual I wish someone had handed me with my discharge papers. If you're looking for someone to motivate you with platitudes, I'm the wrong coach. If you're ready to work to a standard again, alongside men who'll hold you to it — read on.
