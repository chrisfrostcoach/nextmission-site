NET-01 / THE STANDING NET · £20/MO

Join the net.

In uniform you were issued a net without asking: an oppo by default — a battle buddy who clocked when something was off — a section who had your back, a corridor of men who'd already stood where you were standing. Civvy street doesn't issue one — and the gap where it used to be is a tax you pay quietly, in months. The Brotherhood is that net, rebuilt on purpose: section channels, an oppo, two live calls a month. £20 a month, for as long as it earns its place.

Most of the net served. Some are here because someone they love did. Both belong.

NET-02 / WHEN IT GOES WRONG

A net doesn't ask what happened. It catches.

The bad weeks never announce themselves. On a real net you don't have to raise your hand — someone clocks the silence and reaches in. That reach was free in uniform. Here it is rebuilt deliberately, held by men who know the difference between a quiet day and a man going under.

NET-03 / THE RITUALS

The operating rhythm of the net

Named rituals on a fixed cadence. You always know what's next and what's expected — no motivational noise, just a rhythm you can set your week by.

Daily
The net is open. Post the win or the wobble. Either one gets answered — that's the whole point of a net.
Weekly
Orders group. Your weekly SITREP — a short progress report to your section — lands in the section channels; the week's brief goes up for all-call.
Fortnightly
Live call over Zoom — two a month. AARs and obstacles worked in the open. Led, not lectured.
Quarterly
Intake. A new class falls in and takes its numbers on the roll.
NET-04 / MEMBER NUMBER & INSIGNIA

You take a number

Not a username. A number on the roll, issued in order of joining and held for as long as you're on the net.

STANDARD — THE ROLL

Every member takes a number, issued in order of joining. The first intake — Class 26-08 — is marked as founding on the roll, permanently; that mark is never re-issued. The insignia is a real patch, drawn and pressed for members, not a stock graphic — so its plate stays a ruled holding frame until the first patches are in hand and photographed. We don't fake it, and we don't fake you a face to sit next to.

NET-06 / THE PRICE OF ONE ROUND

What £20 actually buys

£20 is a round at the pub — gone by the morning, with nothing to show for it. The same money runs a whole month on a standing net of men who notice when you go quiet and say so. That is the entire pitch. No bonuses, no timers, no theatre — a fair price for a net, paid monthly, cancelled the day it stops earning its place.
